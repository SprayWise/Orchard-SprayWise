New to Fruit Trees?
A beginner's guide to growing fruit and using this app
🌳 Why I Built This
I'm just a regular person who wanted to grow fruit trees. I planted my first trees with zero experience and quickly learned that growing beautiful, healthy fruit is a lot harder than sticking a tree in the ground and waiting. My first years were full of failures — scabby apples, wormy peaches, trees lost to fire blight, fruit destroyed by brown rot overnight. Every time I searched for answers, the information was either too academic, too vague, or buried across dozens of university extension sites that assumed you already knew the terminology.

I couldn't find a single tool that told me what to spray, when to spray it, how much to mix, and what NOT to mix together — all in one place, tailored to what I actually had on hand. So I built one. This app is everything I wish I'd had when I started. Whether you're planting your first apple tree in the backyard or managing a small orchard, hopefully this saves you the years of trial and error it took me to figure it all out.
🍎 What Does This App Do?
In short: it tells you what to spray, when, how much, and warns you when products don't mix safely. Specifically:

Spray Recommendations — Based on your fruit type, current growth stage, weather, and what products you own, the app recommends exactly what to spray right now and calculates the mix rate for your sprayer size.

Weather Integration — Pulls real forecast data and identifies your best spray windows in the next 7 days. It even tells you when rain is coming tomorrow so you can spray today for maximum protection.

Product Inventory — Check off the products you actually own and the app only recommends from those products. It knows which products can be mixed safely and which combinations are dangerous.

Disease & Pest ID — Photo gallery of common fruit tree diseases and pests so you can identify what's attacking your trees and know how to respond.

Spray Log — Track every spray you apply. The app uses your history to calculate when your next spray is due and warns you if you're overdue.
🌱 Beginner Terminology
Growth Stages — Fruit trees go through predictable stages each year: dormant (winter sleep), green tip (first growth), tight cluster (buds forming), bloom (flowers open), petal fall (flowers dropping), then cover sprays through harvest. Different diseases and pests attack at different stages, so spraying at the right stage is everything.

Varieties — The specific "name" of your fruit tree. Just like dogs have breeds, apple trees have varieties — Honeycrisp, Fuji, Gala. Each variety has different disease resistance, flavor, and harvest timing. Knowing your variety helps you spray smarter.

Rootstock — Almost all fruit trees sold at nurseries are actually two trees grafted together. The top part (the variety you eat) is grafted onto a different root system called the rootstock. Rootstock controls how big the tree grows: dwarf (6-10 ft), semi-dwarf (12-18 ft), or standard (25+ ft). It doesn't change the fruit flavor — a Honeycrisp on dwarf rootstock tastes the same as one on standard.

Cross-Pollination — Many fruit trees need pollen from a DIFFERENT variety to set fruit. One Honeycrisp apple tree alone won't produce apples — it needs a different apple variety (like Fuji or Gala) nearby for bees to carry pollen between them. Some trees are "self-fertile" and can pollinate themselves. The Pollination section of this app tells you what each fruit type needs.

Fungicide vs Insecticide — Fungicides kill fungal diseases (scab, rot, mildew). Insecticides kill insects (moths, beetles, maggots). They're completely different products that do completely different jobs. Most fruit trees need BOTH through the growing season. You can often mix them in the same tank — this app's Mix Checker tells you what's safe.

Organic vs Conventional — Organic products (copper, sulfur, neem oil, Spinosad) are derived from natural sources and approved for organic growing. Conventional products (Captan, Immunox, Sevin) are synthetic chemicals. Both work. This app supports both and lets you filter your inventory either way.

PHI (Pre-Harvest Interval) — The number of days you must wait between your last spray and picking the fruit. Captan has a 0-day PHI (spray today, pick tomorrow). Immunox has 14 days. Always check PHI before spraying near harvest — the app shows this in product details.

FRAC / IRAC Groups — Fungicides and insecticides are grouped by how they work. Rotating between different groups prevents diseases and insects from developing resistance. You don't need to memorize these — the app handles it in the recommendations.
🚀 How to Get Started (5 Minutes)
1. Set your location — Tap the 📍 location area at the top and search your zip code or city. This powers the weather forecast and auto-detects your growing stage based on temperature accumulation.

2. Pick your fruit — Tap the fruit tabs (🍎🍐🍑🍒🟣 etc.) to switch between your fruit types. Each fruit has its own spray schedule, diseases, and recommendations.

3. Check your products — Tap the 🧴 My Products bar and check off every spray product you already own. Don't own any yet? Start with just two: a copper fungicide for dormant spray and Captan for the growing season. You can add insecticides later.

4. Follow the recommendations — Open the 🎯 Spray Planner section. The app will tell you exactly what to spray right now based on your stage, weather, and products. Hit the green "Log It" button after each spray.

5. Add your varieties — Go to 🍎 My Varieties and add the specific varieties you're growing. The app has data on 500+ varieties and will auto-fill disease resistance and harvest info.

That's it. The app does the rest — calculating rates, checking compatibility, tracking your schedule, and warning you when you're overdue.
💡 Quick Tips for First-Time Growers
Start small. Two or three trees is plenty to learn on. Don't plant 20 trees your first year.

Dormant spray is the most important spray of the year. Copper + oil applied in late winter before buds open prevents more problems than all the other sprays combined. Don't skip it.

Timing matters more than product. A cheap fungicide applied at the right time beats an expensive one applied too late. This app's growth stage tracking helps you nail the timing.

NEVER spray insecticides during bloom. Bees are pollinating your flowers. Kill the bees and you get zero fruit. The app will warn you about this.

You will lose some fruit. Even commercial orchards lose 10-30% to disease and pests. Don't expect perfection — expect improvement each year as you learn your trees.

Read the label. Every product label tells you the legal rate, what fruits it's registered for, the pre-harvest interval, and safety precautions. The label is the law. This app provides label links for every product.

📖 Detailed Guide
📖 App Layout

The app is organized into four collapsible areas, each with a large arrow button. Tap any area header to expand or collapse it (accordion style — opening one closes the others). All sections start closed — open what you need.

📖 New to Fruit Trees? Start Here

A standalone beginner's guide that appears between the Setup and Spray Program areas. Covers why this app was built, what it does, terminology (growth stages, varieties, rootstock, pollination, fungicide vs insecticide, organic vs conventional, PHI, FRAC), a 5-step quick start, and tips for first-time growers. Tap "Got It" to dismiss permanently. Always accessible again via ❓ Help.

⚙️ My Orchard Setup

The first collapsible area — your personal orchard configuration. Everything here controls what appears in the rest of the app.

🍎 My Fruit Types

Checkbox picker for all 20 fruit types including Apples, Pears, Peaches, Cherries, Plums, Figs, Berries, Pawpaw, Persimmon, Citrus, Grapes, Avocado, Mango, Pomegranate, Olives, Nut Trees, Kiwi, Banana, Jujube, and Guava. Check the fruits you grow — only checked fruits appear in the tab bars in the Spray Program and Reference sections below.

🍎 My Varieties

Add your specific tree varieties (Honeycrisp, Elberta, Montmorency, Hass, Concord, etc.). Browse or search a catalog of 798+ varieties across all 20 fruit types with auto-fill data for many. Each variety shows a custom fruit icon with accurate skin and flesh colors. Has its own linked fruit tabs at the top.

🧴 My Products

73 spray products with search, fruit filter dropdown, filter buttons (All, Mine, Organic, Conventional), and formulation badges (CONC, WDG, WP, RTU, DUST, PREMIX, BAIT). Each product shows fruit compatibility icons for your selected fruits. Check what you own — the app only recommends from your inventory. Each product has a 📋 label link to the official manufacturer/EPA label.

🪣 Sprayer Size

Select your sprayer tank size (1–6 gallons). All product rates automatically calculate both per-gallon and full-tank amounts.

🎯 Spray Program

The second collapsible area — your active spray schedule personalized to your orchard. Has linked fruit tabs at the top. Contains these sections (all individually collapsible):

📚 Yearly Spray Guide

Stage-by-stage overview of what to spray and why for your selected fruit, covering all 20 fruit types from traditional orchards to citrus, grapes, tropical, and nut trees. Key stages marked with ⭐. Includes a "Getting Started?" box with the minimum products for each fruit.

🌱 Growth Stages

Auto-detected via Growing Degree Days (GDD) from your location's temperature data. 8 stages from Dormant/Silver Tip through Post-Harvest. Each fruit tracks independently — peaches lead apples by ~2 weeks. Override manually if needed. Tap "❓ What do these stages mean?" for a visual guide.

🌤 Weather & Forecast

7-day forecast with temperature, rain probability, wind, and spray condition ratings. Scores each day for spray suitability. Best window is typically a dry day before rain.

🎯 Spray Planner

Countdown to next spray, spray-by deadline, Coverage Health Dashboard (season grade, coverage %, gap count, rain exposure), and Top 3 Spray Windows ranked by weather. Shows context when deadline is beyond the 7-day forecast range.

💊 Mix Recommendation

14-day calendar date picker with ★ on best day and red "DUE" on deadline. Select a date to see your recommendation. Shows ✅ Your Top Pick from your inventory, 🏆 Pro Pick popup with commercial upgrades (Indar, Pristine, Altacor, Streptomycin), 🛡️ Curative Backup for missed sprays, and 🔄 Other Options from your inventory.

✏️ Manual Log & 📋 Spray History

Log sprays by selecting date, product, and fruit types. Popup lets you check multiple fruits and optionally specific varieties. Mix details recorded automatically. History shows chronological entries with product, fruits (colored pills), mix rates (🧪), variety detail (🌳), weather, and notes.

📚 Reference & Guides

The third collapsible area — informational resources. Has its own linked fruit tabs. Starts closed. Contains:

🔍 Disease & Pest ID Gallery

Visual identification cards filtered by your selected fruit. Tap any entry for full symptoms, timing, and control info.

🌱 Rootstock Guide

30+ rootstocks across apples, pears, stone fruits, citrus, grapes, avocados, and nut trees. Size classes, bearing age, cold hardiness, spray impact, and practical recommendations. Fruits without grafting traditions show propagation guides instead.

🦌 Wildlife · 🐝 Pollination · 📐 Converter · ⚠️ Mix Rules

Wildlife threats (8 animals with fruit-specific severity and disease connections), pollination requirements and pollinator safety rules, unit converter (38 pairs for spray mixing), and product mixing compatibility checker with 88 rules and 30 fruit restrictions.

📝 Orchard Notes

The fourth collapsible area — your personal orchard journal. Create, edit, and organize notes with photos.

✏️ Creating Notes

Tap ✏️ New Note to open the editor. Add a title, write your observations or plans in the text area, and optionally attach up to 6 photos per note. Notes are automatically dated when created. Tap 💾 Save Note to save.

📋 Viewing & Managing Notes

Saved notes appear sorted newest-first with title, creation date, and a text preview. Each note has ✏️ Edit and 🗑️ Delete buttons. Editing a note preserves the original creation date and adds a "modified" timestamp. Photos can be tapped to view full-screen. Notes are included in data exports and can be restored from backups.

🌡️ Header

The top bar has: 🌡️ Zone (search location by city/ZIP, auto-detects USDA zone), 📍 Location, ⚙️ Settings (export/import data, vibration, sound, weekend/weekday spray priority, install as app), and ❓ Help.

🧭 Bottom Navigation Bar

The dark nav bar at the bottom has 7 quick-jump buttons: Spray (recommendations & planner), ID (disease/pest gallery), Guide (yearly spray guide), Log (spray history & manual entry), Varieties (your collection), Convert (unit converter), and Rules (mixing compatibility & checker). Tapping any button scrolls to that section and opens it.

✏️ Customization

Tap the orchard name at the top to rename your orchard — it's your app. Use ⚙️ to toggle haptic feedback and sounds on/off. Set your sprayer size (1–6 gallons) — it's remembered between sessions. All your settings, products, varieties, and spray logs are stored locally on your device and never sent to any server.

📲 Install as App (PWA)

Tap 📲 Install App in the settings bar to add this to your home screen as a standalone app. On Chrome/Edge it uses the native install prompt. On iPhone/iPad, use Safari's Share button → "Add to Home Screen." Once installed, it opens full-screen like a native app. Requires HTTPS hosting (GitHub Pages, Netlify, etc.) to activate the install feature.

💾 Backup & Export

Tap ⚙️ in the header to export all your data as a JSON file or import a previous backup. The export includes your spray log, orchard notes, per-fruit stages, product inventory, varieties, and all settings. All data is stored locally in your browser — nothing is sent to any server. Back up periodically in case you clear browser data!
